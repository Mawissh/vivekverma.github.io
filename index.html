About

Background, education and research focus

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Mathematics at the Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee, working under the supervision of Dr. Sanjeev Kumar. My doctoral research focuses on developing classical and quantum algorithms for secure multimedia data encryption and watermarking.

My work sits at the intersection of chaos theory, memristive systems, and quantum computing. I design novel image encryption schemes using chaotic maps, Hopfield neural networks, and quantum-inspired unitary operators. A central thread of my research is the security analysis of these systems against both classical and quantum adversaries.

In post-quantum cryptography, I work on lattice-based cryptographic constructions — studying hard lattice problems such as LWE and SIS, analysing their proofs of hardness, and designing new problem formulations. I am also exploring attribute-based encryption (ABE) schemes that remain secure in a post-quantum world, with an interest in expressive access control structures and their efficiency under lattice assumptions.

I have been recognised with the ANRF Travel Grant (Govt. of India) and a Student Research Scholarship from QIP 2026, and I have served as a Trusted Reviewer for IOP Publishing journals including Physica Scripta and Journal of Optics.
